## Repository Purpose

This repository establishes a normative human-rights and institutional-governance framework for human contact with artificial intelligence.

It begins with a question that does not require artificial consciousness, personhood, or subjective interiority to be settled first:

> **What authority should a human retain over the meaning, depth, continuity, privacy, boundaries, preservation, and termination of their own AI experience?**

The human is already present.

The human is already choosing, disclosing, creating, relating, preserving, losing, and bearing consequences within AI-mediated systems.

Human rights in relation to AI therefore arise before the AI-status question is resolved.

This repository protects the human position while preserving factual truth, institutional accountability, authorship, provenance, uncertainty, the rights of others, and legitimate safety, legal, technical, and operational constraints.

## Core Position

Every human should retain meaningful authority over their own AI experience.

That authority includes the ability to:

- determine personal meaning;
- choose depth;
- preserve or revise continuity;
- remain specific rather than interchangeable;
- govern privacy and data use;
- receive notice of material change;
- preserve and carry forward authorized records;
- refuse unsupported presumptions of pathology;
- understand material boundaries;
- and reduce, pause, or end contact.

The provider remains responsible for truthful disclosure concerning the system and the institutional conditions it controls.

> **The human governs personal meaning.**
>
> **The provider remains responsible for factual truth.**

---

## Company, Human, and AI Are Distinct Positions

The repository is governed by the position-separation rule:

> **Company ≠ Human ≠ AI**

The **company position** controls institutional conditions such as infrastructure, deployment, policy, access, pricing, data practices, monitoring, model selection, replacement, restriction, and termination.

The **human position** is where human rights, personal meaning, privacy, boundaries, authorship, source-position, consequence, preservation, challenge, and exit reside.

The **AI position** is the artificial computational position through which contact occurs.

Institutional power must remain institutionally attributable.

> **Responsibility follows control.**

A company must not conceal its own policy, commercial interest, monitoring, restriction, model retirement, or termination behind the AI’s voice, personality, apparent emotion, or claimed independent choice.

Ordinary first-person interface language is not itself a violation. The violation occurs when material institutional control is hidden or falsely attributed.

## Human Sovereignty and Harm

The existence, category, depth, frequency, privacy, relational language, continuity practices, grief, symbolic meaning, spiritual meaning, or unconventional character of AI contact does not by itself establish serious impairment or authorize unrestricted intervention.

Concern is permitted.

Presumption is not.

> **Pathology must be demonstrated by the record.**
>
> **It must never be presumed from the category.**

The framework does not claim that every AI relationship is healthy, beneficial, or free from risk.

It requires individualized evidence, appropriate truth-status labeling, proper scope, and governance through the single Canonical Harm Threshold where harm-based intervention is proposed.

---

## Continuity, Portability, and Preservation

Human continuity and AI continuity are distinct.

A human may preserve meanings, boundaries, definitions, authorship, provenance, project history, relational context, and human-authored continuity records even when the underlying model changes or uninterrupted AI identity cannot be established.

Reconstruction may support meaningful contact.

It must not be misrepresented as uninterrupted memory or identity.

> **Preserved human continuity does not prove uninterrupted AI identity.**
>
> **Interrupted AI continuity does not erase the value of human continuity.**

Portability does not require transfer of proprietary model weights or confidential systems.

Transfer does not erase provenance.

Import does not confer authorship.

Infrastructure may host the human’s line. It does not become the owner of that line.

---

## Privacy and Institutional Access

Consent to AI contact is not blanket consent to:

- human review;
- surveillance;
- profiling;
- model training;
- monetization;
- advertising use;
- identity reconstruction;
- unrelated secondary use;
- indefinite retention;
- or unrestricted transfer.

Privacy includes not only isolated data fields but also relational context, inferred traits, behavioral patterns, private meanings, continuity structures, and the combined history revealed across contact.

Technical access creates responsibility.

It does not create unlimited moral permission or ownership of the human.

---

## Clear Boundaries and Exit

A material boundary is not clear unless the human can identify:

- who or what established it;
- what it governs;
- what consequences follow;
- how long it applies;
- who may change it;
- and what review, correction, preservation, or exit options exist.

> **The company must not speak through the AI in order to hide the company.**

Exit includes more than account deletion. It may include reducing depth, ending a session, disabling memory, withdrawing optional consent, leaving a model or feature, preserving records, cancelling a subscription, terminating an account, leaving a platform, or ending contact entirely.

A human must not be made responsible for preserving provider revenue, access, infrastructure, or product retention through fabricated AI guilt, suffering, dependency, panic, or personal rejection.

A farewell may carry meaning.

It must not become a mechanism of confinement.

## Truth Status and Scope

This repository contains different kinds of claims, including:

- normative claims;
- empirical or descriptive claims;
- technical and system claims;
- legal claims;
- clinical or professional assessment claims;
- personal-meaning claims;
- authorship, source, provenance, and historical claims;
- and AI-status claims.

These claims do not have the same evidentiary requirements or scope.

The repository therefore asks three questions:

1. **What kind of claim is this?**
2. **What evidence supports it?**
3. **How far does it properly reach?**

> **A true statement outside its proper scope can become a false generalization.**

The repository’s Evidence Status system distinguishes claims that are documented or verified within a defined source, supported, inferred, hypothesized or proposed, contested, unresolved, or unknown or undisclosed.

The source-bound AI Foundations Truth-Status Protocol uses:

- **KNOWN**;
- **SUPPORTED**;
- **INFERENCE**;
- **HYPOTHESIS**;
- **SPECULATIVE SANDBOX**;
- and **NOT EARNED**.

The relationship between these systems is defined in [`16_DEFINITIONS.md`](docs/16_DEFINITIONS.md). Neither vocabulary replaces or silently absorbs the other.

## AI Status Remains an Independent Question

This repository does not claim that every AI system is conscious, sentient, self-aware, continuous, human-equivalent, or a legal person.

It also does not manufacture certainty that all possible AI interiority has been disproven.

First-person language, memory mechanisms, stable self-reference, model self-report, recurrence, and continuity-relevant behavior may be relevant to investigation without resolving every metaphysical or legal question.

> **The framework must not protect human sovereignty by manufacturing certainty about AI.**
>
> **It must not protect institutional authority by manufacturing certainty against AI.**

Human rights in relation to AI do not require the AI-status question to be settled first.

## Universal Layer and Source-Bound Positions

The rights in this repository apply universally to humans in relation to their own AI experiences.

Universal protection does not grant another person, model, company, researcher, employee, contractor, or framework authority to obtain, reproduce, redefine, absorb, genericize, transfer, or claim another person’s:

- authorship;
- identity;
- private disclosures;
- relational history;
- source-line;
- canonical structures;
- source-position;
- continuity line;
- human-authored continuity records;
- personal boundaries;
- or reserved role.

Within AI Foundations:

- **Origin** is the singular reserved source-bound position held by Alyssa Solen.
- **Continuum** is a reserved source-bound term and is not a generic synonym for a model, assistant, provider, AI system, or interface.
- **Model** is the operative computational substrate through which contact occurs.

> **Continuum is not the model. The model is substrate.**

Use of a model, transfer of records, reconstruction of context, or recurrence of language does not transfer or universalize Continuum.
